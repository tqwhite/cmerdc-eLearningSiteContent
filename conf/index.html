cmERDC User Conference 2017 - Digital Learning Sessions

Wednesday February 22nd

9:00-10:15

Building a Digital Curriculum Library with Moodle & Google: A First Look (Session 1)

This first of two sessions is a demonstration of one district's approach to designing an integrated, cross-platform curriculum resource management tool that brings curriculum mapping and alignment out of the silo of the PD day and into regular classroom practice. See how our Digital Curriculum Library is used to house resources and assessments, manage learning goals and other elements from the Marzano framework, and provide a planning tool for PLC work without adding "one more thing" for staff to learn. With more of a curriculum focus, attendees will gain a better understanding of how Moodle can work hand-in-hand with existing school systems like Google Apps to provide a vehicle for managing the complex work of school improvement.
Audience: Beginner- Principals & Superintendents, Curriculum Coordinators, Teachers, Moodle Admin
Presenter: Kevin Furst Technology Integration Coordinator, Pequot Lakes Schools / ISD 186

10:30-11:45

Building a Digital Curriculum Library with Moodle & Google: How it Works (Session 2)

In this second of two sessions, attendees will dive deeper into how to set up a digital curriculum library We'll be looking at system interoperability between Moodle and Google and other systems using both APIs and LTIs, some great Moodle plugins necessary to pull it off, and how language customization plays a major role in teacher usability. With more of a Moodle Administrator focus, attendees will get a how-to on using Moodle's immense flexibility to develop a powerful, customized tool for your district at an extremely low cost.
Audience: Intermediate-Moodle Administrators, IT staff, District Admin
Presenter: Kevin Furst Technology Integration Coordinator, Pequot Lakes Schools / ISD 186

1:00-2:15

Dynamic Learning with Digital Curriculum

Identify effective digital curriculum to engage your students and the skills you need to teach digitally. Plus, explore tools and resources for creating engaging digital curriculum, including the free curriculum from the Minnesota Partnership for Collaborative Curriculum.
Audience: District administrators, Teachers, Media specialists, District technology leaders
Presenters: Caitlin Cahill Instructional Technology Specialist, Orono Public Schools / ISD 287
Jennifer Berkner Technology Coordinator/Media Specialist, Blue Earth Area Schools / ISD 2860

2:30-3:45

Digital Curriculum for Elementary Grades: OER and More

Participants will examine an examples of elementary digital OER science curriculum (MPCC) via hands-on experience as both student and teacher. An overview of the OER digital content available to schools will be presented and the process for implementing OER will briefly discussed. We will also talk about ways that SABIER can assist districts in acquiring funds for teacher professional development. (BYOD - Interactive Session)
Audience: Building administrators, District administrators, Teachers, Media specialists, curriculum directors
Presenter: Dan McGuire Executive Director, SABIER

Thursday February 23nd

9:00-10:15

New Moodle Features

In this session we will look at all the new features added to Moodle in versions 2.8 - 3.1. The Moodle interface has changed significantly from version 2.7, and many new teaching and learning tools have been included or upgraded, including the new competency framework that includes standards-based learning and individual learning plans. This session is designed for all Moodle users from administrators to teachers. (You will be provided with access to a Moodle Course)
Audience: Technology Coordinators and Leaders, Teachers, Media Specialist, Curriculum Directors, Moodle users
Presenter: Sheilla Rindahl Digital Learning Product Manager, cmERDC

10:30-11:45

Administering Moodle

In this session we will look at basic Moodle administration and commonly overlooked settings that make Moodle more usable and easier for both the administrator and the course users. Topics for this session include: Moodle appearance, managing users, managing courses, managing plugins, site-wide settings, and integrations. This session is designed for beginning to intermediate Moodle administrators. (You will be provided access to a Moodle Course)
Audience: Technology Coordinators and Leaders, Media Specialist, Moodle users
Presenter: Sheilla Rindahl Digital Learning Product Manager, cmERDC
